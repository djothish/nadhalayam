Our Classes

Eleven disciplines. One philosophy. Every student deserves to find their voice.

Bharatanatyam

From basic adavus to full margam performances. Emphasis on precise footwork, expressive abhinaya, and understanding the stories behind each piece.

Mohiniyattam

Kerala's own classical dance form — characterized by its fluid, wave-like movements and deeply emotive storytelling. Taught with reverence for the lasya tradition.

Kuchippudi

The graceful classical dance form from Andhra Pradesh — characterized by its storytelling, fluid movements, and intricate footwork. Taught with reverence for the tradition and emphasis on expressive abhinaya.

Carnatic Music

The heart of Nadhalayam. Students learn from the fundamentals of sarali varisai through varnams, kritis, and manodharma sangeetham — all taught in the guru-shishya tradition.

Keyboard

Structured piano-keyboard training covering Western classical, Indian film music, and Carnatic adaptations. Suitable for all ages and levels.

Guitar

From open chords to fingerstyle, lead, and Indian classical fusion. Acoustic and electric. Whether you want to strum film songs or compose — we'll get you there.

Violin

Carnatic violin in the traditional style, plus Western violin for those interested in both worlds. Bowing technique, gamakas, and ensemble playing.

Veena

The queen of Indian instruments. Students learn the intricate techniques of meettu and gamaka that make the veena sing — from basic geethams to complex kritis.

Tabla

Master the taal. From basic bols to advanced compositions, students develop rhythmic precision and the ability to accompany both classical and light music.

Mridangam

The primary rhythmic accompaniment for Carnatic music. Students learn the art of following a concert, playing thani avarthanam, and understanding the mathematics of laya.

Flute

The bamboo flute — one of the oldest instruments in Indian classical music. Breath control, fingering techniques, and the meditative beauty of this instrument taught step by step.

Painting

From Kerala mural art to contemporary styles — a space for visual creativity. Students explore color, form, and expression through guided practice and personal projects.
